What is The Shisei Method?

Method

Shisei embodies the art of refined movement, blending grace and strength. Founder and former ballerina Mai Suzuki developed the Method over countless high-octane performances. Experience the Method over personalized 1:1 physical conditioning sessions that suits all body types.

Approach

A ballerina meticulously prepares for a performance. Likewise, our approach begins with joint mobilization, core exercises, and coordination strengthening workouts. We integrate these moments from a ballerina's routine into your everyday life.

Practices

To improve your physical performance, we focus on posture, tension release, and a stronger core. We share daily practices for you to improve your quality of life through better Ballerina posture.

The process

Step 1.

Counselling

During our initial consultation, we'll engage in a comprehensive discussion to gain insights into your body's needs. Sharing your injury history and current physical condition is crucial for us to tailor our approach effectively.

Step 2.

Posture Analysis

Using posture boards, capturing images to assess your condition, we'll pinpoint key body landmarks, providing a visual representation of your posture for easy understanding.

Step 3.

Release

We choose highly effective release techniques such as the Imphy fascia release stick, Japan PNF Technique Association and Mai’s Ballet-inspired body conditioning technique to mobilize joints too. Reducing tension is key to make our exercises more effective, a fundamental part of the Shisei methods approach.

Step 4.

Exercise

Our Ballet-inspired Pilates based exercise philosophy revolves around activating the appropriate muscle groups to gain control over your body. If we detect any discomfort or suboptimal form, we adjust the exercise intensity accordingly to ensure a safe and effective session.

Step 5.

Review

We assign personalized homework at the end of each session. Through visual assessment using body landmarks and photographs, we track your progress and provide you with a session chart detailing today's activities and your assigned homework. This comprehensive approach allows for a deeper understanding of your body and facilitates faster improvement.

Our Team

Amelia Stanley

Physiotherapist

Amelia Stanley

Physiotherapist

Amelia graduated with BSc (Hons) Physiotherapy and has worked as a fully registered physiotherapist in various clinical settings in both the hospital and private practice. Amelia is certified in Dry Needling and is also certified to teach Polestar Pilates Rehabilitation, Level 1 GYROTONIC®️, Progressing Ballet Technique, and BarreConcept.

Her interest is in injury prevention and rehabilitation for dancers, having trained in ballet, Chinese dance, jazz, contemporary and tap herself. She has provided on-site physiotherapy services for numerous local and international performing arts groups including Company Wayne McGregor and Cirque du Soleil, and has conducted several dance rehabilitation workshops.
